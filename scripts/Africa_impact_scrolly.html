Millions of livestock have died across the Horn of Africa this year, where over 15 million people face severe water shortages, food insecurity and historic drought.

It is the African continent’s 152nd drought since 2000

Low-income countries are the most at risk from climate change. They are both highly vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and the least prepared to address them.

African countries are particularly susceptible to climate change. The world’s 10 most climate vulnerable countries are in Africa, accounting for 20% of the continent’s population. Most African countries are also unprepared to tackle the impacts of climate change.
