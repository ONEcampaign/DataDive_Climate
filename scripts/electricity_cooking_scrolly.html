548 million Africans (46%) do not have access to electricity. Africa’s 54 countries collectively consumed less electricity in 2019 than Japan, with a population more than 10 times larger. Additionally, outages and load shedding shave around 2% off of Africa’s annual GDP due to business disruption and lost profits.

At current projections, over 65% of the population in sub-Saharan Africa will still rely on woodfuel for cooking in 2050.
